## 2. Exercise Content (Module 1 — Full Detail)

Each lesson has 6-10 exercises. Below is the full exercise content for Module 1 (the first module a user plays). Other modules follow the same structure.

### Lesson 1.1: Your First Prompt (6 exercises — first-lesson, easy mode)

**Concept Intro Card:**
> A prompt is just an instruction you give to AI. The clearer your instruction, the better AI's response. Think of it like briefing a smart assistant — vague instructions get vague results.
>
> **Bad prompt:** "Help me with work stuff"
> **Good prompt:** "Write a short paragraph explaining why our team should adopt weekly standups"

**Exercise 1 — Output Judge (Recognition)**
- Instruction: "Which response came from a better prompt?"
- Output A: "Here are some general tips for being productive at work: wake up early, make a to-do list, take breaks..."
- Output B: "Here's a 3-step plan to reduce your team's meeting time by 30% this month: 1) Audit recurring meetings..."
- Correct: B
- Explanation: "Output B is specific and actionable because the prompt gave AI a clear task. Vague prompts get vague answers."

**Exercise 2 — Fill-in-the-Blank (Recognition)**
- Instruction: "Complete this prompt to get a useful response"
- Template: "Write a _____ about _____ for _____"
- Blanks: [short paragraph] [why remote work improves retention] [my manager]
- Word bank: "short paragraph", "something", "why remote work improves retention", "stuff about work", "my manager", "someone"
- Correct: "short paragraph", "why remote work improves retention", "my manager"
- Explanation: "Specific details in your prompt — what format, what topic, who it's for — make the output useful."

**Exercise 3 — Real-World Scenario (Guided Practice)**
- Scenario: "You need to send an email to a client explaining a project delay. Which prompt would you use?"
- Option A: "Write an email about a delay"
- Option B: "Write a professional email to a client explaining that our website redesign is delayed by 2 weeks due to a vendor issue. Keep the tone apologetic but confident."
- Option C: "Help me with this email thing"
- Correct: B
- Explanation: "Prompt B gives AI everything it needs: the format (email), the audience (client), the situation (delay, reason), and the tone (apologetic but confident)."

**Exercise 4 — Prompt Builder (Guided Practice)**
- Instruction: "Arrange these into an effective prompt"
- Components (shuffled): "Write a", "professional email", "to my team", "announcing our new flexible work policy", "in a positive and encouraging tone"
- Correct order: "Write a" → "professional email" → "to my team" → "announcing our new flexible work policy" → "in a positive and encouraging tone"
- Explanation: "A good prompt has structure: what to create, who it's for, what it's about, and what tone to use."

**Exercise 5 — Fill-in-the-Blank (Application)**
- Instruction: "You're a freelancer who needs to write a project proposal. Complete this prompt."
- Template: "Write a _____ for a potential client who needs a _____. Include _____ and keep it _____."
- Word bank: "one-page proposal", "website redesign", "timeline and pricing", "under 500 words"
- Correct: all four
- Explanation: "You just built a prompt that could save you 30 minutes of writing. That's the power of good prompting."

**Exercise 6 — Real-World Scenario (Application)**
- Scenario: "Your boss asks you to quickly draft talking points for a presentation tomorrow. You have 5 minutes. What do you do?"
- Option A: "Open AI and type 'help me with a presentation'"
- Option B: "Open AI and type 'Create 5 concise talking points for a 10-minute presentation about Q1 sales results to our executive team. Focus on wins and one area for improvement.'"
- Option C: "Don't use AI — write it yourself so it sounds authentic"
- Correct: B
- Explanation: "A specific prompt gets you a strong first draft in seconds. You can then edit it to add your personal touch. That's faster AND more authentic than either extreme."

### Lesson 1.2: Add Context (8 exercises)

**Concept Intro Card:**
> Context is the secret weapon of great prompts. When you tell AI the background — who you are, who it's for, what's already happened — the output goes from generic to surprisingly useful.
>
> **Without context:** "Write a follow-up email"
> **With context:** "Write a follow-up email to a potential client I met at a marketing conference last week. We discussed their need for social media strategy. I want to schedule a call."

**Exercise 1 — Output Judge**
- Instruction: "Which email was written with better context in the prompt?"
- Output A: "Dear Sir/Madam, I am writing to follow up on our recent interaction. I would like to explore potential synergies..."
- Output B: "Hi Sarah, It was great meeting you at MarketingWorld last Thursday. I loved your take on TikTok strategy for B2B — it got me thinking about some ideas for Acme Corp's social presence. Would you have 30 minutes next week to chat?"
- Correct: B
- Explanation: "Output B is personal and specific because the prompt included names, events, topics, and goals. Context turns AI from a generic assistant into one that knows the situation."

**Exercise 2 — Fill-in-the-Blank**
- Template: "I'm a _____ who needs to _____. The audience is _____. The goal is to _____."
- Word bank: "freelance graphic designer", "write a cold outreach email", "small business owners who need branding", "book a discovery call"
- Correct: all four
- Explanation: "This 'I am / I need / the audience is / the goal is' framework works for almost any prompt."

**Exercise 3 — Real-World Scenario**
- Scenario: "You're writing a prompt to get AI to help with a blog post. Which context would improve the output the most?"
- Option A: "My blog is about productivity"
- Option B: "I run a productivity blog for remote workers. My readers are managers at mid-size companies. The blog post should be practical, not theoretical, and around 800 words."
- Option C: "I need a blog post"
- Correct: B
- Explanation: "The more context AI has — your niche, your audience, their level, the format — the more the output sounds like YOU wrote it."

**Exercise 4 — Prompt Builder**
- Components (shuffled): "I'm a small business owner", "who runs a coffee shop", "Write me a response", "to a negative Google review", "that's professional and empathetic", "without admitting fault"
- Correct order: "I'm a small business owner" → "who runs a coffee shop" → "Write me a response" → "to a negative Google review" → "that's professional and empathetic" → "without admitting fault"
- Explanation: "You included who you are, what you need, and HOW you want it — that's context-rich prompting."

**Exercise 5 — Output Judge**
- Instruction: "Both prompts ask AI to write a LinkedIn post. Which prompt gave better context?"
- Output A: "Excited to share that I've been promoted! Hard work pays off. #career #growth"
- Output B: "After 3 years of building our customer success team from 2 to 15 people, I'm thrilled to step into the VP of Customer Experience role at Nexon. The biggest lesson? Hiring people smarter than you isn't threatening — it's the fastest path to impact."
- Correct: B
- Explanation: "Output B came from a prompt that included specifics: the timeline, the accomplishment, the company name, and a personal insight. That's what makes content feel real."

**Exercise 6 — Fill-in-the-Blank**
- Template: "Context: I'm _____. I need _____. Background: _____. Please write _____ that _____."
- Word bank: "a marketing manager at a SaaS startup", "to announce a product update to customers", "we just launched dark mode, our most-requested feature", "an email", "sounds excited but not over-the-top"
- Correct: all five
- Explanation: "The Context → Need → Background → Request pattern works for almost anything."

**Exercise 7 — Real-World Scenario**
- Scenario: "A colleague sends you a prompt that says 'Summarize this report.' What would you add to make it better?"
- Option A: "Summarize this report in 3 bullet points, focusing on budget implications for Q2"
- Option B: "Summarize this report better"
- Option C: "Please summarize"
- Correct: A
- Explanation: "You added format (bullet points), focus area (budget), and timeframe (Q2). Three small additions, dramatically better output."

**Exercise 8 — Prompt Builder**
- Components: "You are an experienced HR consultant", "Review this job description", "for a senior product designer role", "at a mid-stage startup", "Suggest improvements", "to attract more diverse candidates"
- Correct: all in order shown
- Explanation: "You gave AI a role (HR consultant), a task (review), context (startup, senior role), and a goal (diversity). That's an expert-level prompt."
